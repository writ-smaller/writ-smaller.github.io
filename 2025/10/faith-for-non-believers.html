What if what you believe isn't the point?

The word covenant appears throughout scripture, but few stop to ask what it truly means. We treat it as a synonym for promise or contract, but what if it’s something deeper—something relational rather than transactional?

In Hebrew, the word is berit (בְּרִית). It doesn’t merely describe an agreement; it marks a binding relationship, sealed often in blood, grounded not in law but in life. To break a covenant isn’t to break a rule—it’s to break trust.

So, when modern readers see God’s covenant, do they imagine a contract—or a kinship?
